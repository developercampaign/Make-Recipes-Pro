Sauerbraten (Marinated Pot Roast)

  • Time: 52 Mins
  • Serving: 04 People

Ingredients

  • 2 cups red wine vinegar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 10 whole black peppercorns
  • 5 whole cloves

Instructions:

Prepare the Marinade

  • In a large bowl, combine the red wine vinegar, water, red wine, sliced onion, carrots, celery, garlic, peppercorns, cloves, bay leaves, and sugar. Stir well to dissolve the sugar.
  • Place the beef roast in a large, non-reactive container (glass or ceramic is best). Pour the marinade over the roast, making sure it is completely submerged. Cover and refrigerate for 3 to 5 days, turning the roast once or twice each day to ensure even marination.

Cook the Roast:

  • Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  • Remove the roast from the marinade, pat it dry with paper towels, and season it generously with salt and pepper.
  • In a large Dutch oven or oven-safe pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Sear the roast on all sides until it is well browned, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the roast and set it aside.
  • Strain the marinade, discarding the solids and reserving the liquid.
  • Pour the marinade into the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Add the beef broth and bring to a simmer.
  • Return the roast to the pot, cover, and transfer to the preheated oven. Braise for about 3 to 4 hours, or until the meat is very tender.

Make the Sauce:

  • Remove the roast from the pot and keep it warm.
  • Place the pot over medium heat and bring the cooking liquid to a simmer.
  • In a small bowl, mix the flour and butter to form a paste (this is called a beurre manié).
  • Whisk the beurre manié into the simmering liquid to thicken the sauce. If desired, add the crushed gingersnaps for additional thickening and flavor.
  • Cook for a few more minutes, stirring constantly, until the sauce has thickened to your liking.
