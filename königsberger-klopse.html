Königsberger Klopse

  • Time: 28 Mins
  • Serving: 03 People

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 6 cups beef or vegetable broth (for simmering)

Instructions:

Prepare the Marinade

  • In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, chopped onion, breadcrumbs, egg, milk, mustard, parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed.
  • Shape the mixture into small meatballs, about the size of a walnut.
  • In a large pot, bring the broth to a gentle simmer. Add the meatballs in batches, ensuring they are not overcrowded. Simmer the meatballs for about 15-20 minutes until they are cooked through.
  • Remove the meatballs from the broth with a slotted spoon and set them aside. Reserve 2 cups of the broth for the sauce.

Make the Sauce:

  • In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour to form a roux and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture is lightly golden.
  • Gradually whisk in the reserved broth, ensuring there are no lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it starts to thicken.
  • Stir in the heavy cream, lemon juice, and capers. Season with salt, pepper, and sugar (if using). Cook the sauce for another 2-3 minutes until it reaches a smooth, creamy consistency.

Combine and Serve:

  • Return the meatballs to the sauce, gently stirring to coat them evenly. Simmer for a few more minutes to heat the meatballs through.
  • Serve the Königsberger Klopse hot, garnished with extra parsley if desired.
